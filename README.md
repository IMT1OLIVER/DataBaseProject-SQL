Oto zaktualizowane streszczenie w języku angielskim, uwzględniające Twoją rolę jako kierownika projektu:As the project manager and a co-author of this initiative at the Łódź University of Technology for the 2025/2026 academic year, I led my team in developing a comprehensive database system designed to manage a modern bowling alley's operations. My primary objective in overseeing this project was to engineer a structure that not only organizes customer data and lane availability but also precisely maps the dynamic progression of bowling matches. I focused on ensuring full data consistency across all modules, from the initial reservation request and resource verification to the final recording of sports results in the client’s activity history.Within the system architecture, I defined key modules covering reservations, payments, and facility infrastructure. Each reservation is treated as a unique event assigned to a specific lane and customer, with the price being automatically calculated based on active price lists and applicable discounts. I devoted significant attention to modeling the gameplay itself, dividing it into ten rounds and individual throws, which allows for the generation of advanced player statistics, including strikes, spares, and fouls.During the implementation phase in MySQL, I decided to move away from natural identifiers in favor of surrogate keys to significantly accelerate database performance and simplify table joins. I applied rigorous integrity rules using foreign keys—for instance, deleting a reservation cascades to clear all associated throws, while the removal of a payment method currently in use is strictly restricted. To reduce the load on the application layer, I implemented business logic, such as automatically blocking lanes marked for maintenance and calculating discounts, directly within database triggers.I also ensured security and access control by introducing a role-based permission system linked to specific job positions. In my system, an operator handles daily game registration, a manager oversees pricing and lane availability, and an administrator controls system logs and employee accounts. This architecture allows for flexible personnel management and protects sensitive data from unauthorized access, which is crucial for the daily functioning of the facility.Although the system effectively manages reservation and sporting processes, I recognize areas for further development. Currently, the database does not include a warehouse module or bar billing, which I plan to implement in the future alongside integration with a mobile application. Nevertheless, the created solution provides a solid engineering foundation that guarantees high data integrity and a full reflection of gameplay, fulfilling the primary design goals of my team.
